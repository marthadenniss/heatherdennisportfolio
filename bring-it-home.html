Bringing It Home: Alanna Lynise

By Heather Denniss
The Blade | hdenniss@theblade.com
September 15, 2024 — 10:15 AM

Alanna Lynise, a gifted dancer and community advocate from Toledo, has spent the past decade inspiring young people through her work in arts education and outreach programs.

Her passion for dance began early, studying ballet and jazz as a child, and later expanding her repertoire to contemporary and hip-hop styles. She has performed regionally and nationally, often incorporating themes of identity and resilience into her choreography.

In addition to her artistic pursuits, Alanna is the founder of “Dance Forward,” a nonprofit organization dedicated to making dance education accessible to underprivileged youth in Northwest Ohio.

Giving Back Through Art

Dance Forward offers free weekly classes, workshops, and performance opportunities to over 150 young dancers annually. The program also collaborates with local schools and community centers to reach a broad audience.

Alanna’s work has earned her numerous awards, including the Toledo Arts Council’s Emerging Artist Award and the Ohio Governor’s Volunteer Service Award.

Community Impact

Through her leadership, Dance Forward has grown into a vibrant hub for creativity and empowerment. Many alumni have gone on to pursue professional dance careers or arts-related higher education.

Alanna believes that art has the power to transform lives and build stronger communities. “Dance teaches discipline, confidence, and communication,” she says. “It helps young people find their voice and tell their story.”
